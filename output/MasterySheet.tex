\textbf{Applied Complex Analysis (2021)}

\section{Mastery Sheet}
\rule{\textwidth}{1pt}
This problem sheet is about the relationship between truncated Chebyshev expansions and Chebyshev interpolants. If a function $f$ is analytic on $[-1, 1]$, it can be expanded as a Chebyshev series:

\[
f = \sum_{k = 0}^{\infty}c_kT_k(x).
\]
A truncated Chebyshev expansion $f_n(x)$ is an approximation to $f$ obtained by truncating the Chebyshev series after $n$ terms

\[
\sum_{k = 0}^{n-1}c_k T_k(x)=:f_n(x),
\]
see Lecture 19. A Chebyshev interpolant $p_n(x)$ interpolates $f$ at the roots of $T_n(x)$,

\[
x_k = \cos\left( {2k-1\over 2n} \pi  \right), \qquad k = 1, \ldots, n,
\]
i.e.,

\[
p_n(x_k) = f(x_k), \qquad k = 1, \ldots, n.
\]
Since $p_n$ is a polynomial of degree $n-1$,

\[
p_n(x) = \sum_{k = 0}^{n-1}a_kT_k(x)
\]
\begin{itemize}
\item[1. ] Give a formula for the coefficients $c_k$.


\item[2. ] Show that for $k = 1, \ldots, n$,

\end{itemize}
\[
T_m(x_k) = -T_{2n \pm m}(x_k) = T_{4n \pm m}(x_k) = -T_{6n \pm m}(x_k)= T_{8n \pm m}(x_k) = -T_{10n \pm m}(x_k) = \cdots,
\]
for $0 \leq m \leq n$.

\begin{itemize}
\item[3. ] Show that

\end{itemize}
\[
a_0 = \sum_{j = 0}^{\infty}c_{2jn}(-1)^j
\]
and for $1 \leq m \leq n-1$

\[
a_m = c_m + \sum_{j = 1}^{\infty}(-1)^{j}\left[ c_{2jn + m}  +  c_{2jn - m}\right].
\]
\begin{itemize}
\item[4. ] Show that

\end{itemize}
\[
p_n(x) - f(x)  = \sum_{k = 0}^{n-1}\left(a_k - c_k\right)T_k(x) - (f(x) - f_n(x)).
\]
\begin{itemize}
\item[5. ] Obtain an upper bound on $\vert p_n(x) - f(x) \vert$ for $x \in [-1, 1]$.

\end{itemize}
